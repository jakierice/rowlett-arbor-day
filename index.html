Bur Oak

Planting

The Bur Oak is a hardy tree that thrives in a range of soil types. It's best to plant in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Care

Water regularly during the first few years of growth. Once established, Bur Oaks are drought-tolerant. Prune during the dormant season to maintain shape.

Bald Cypress

Planting

Plant Bald Cypress in wet or moist areas. They are particularly well-suited for swampy conditions but can also adapt to drier soils.

Care

Requires regular watering. Mulch around the base to retain moisture. Minimal pruning is needed, mainly to remove dead or damaged branches.

Cedar Elm

Planting

Cedar Elms prefer full sun and well-drained soil. They are adaptable to various soil types but grow best in a slightly acidic to neutral pH.

Care

Water regularly during dry periods. Cedar Elms are relatively low-maintenance. Prune to remove any crossing branches and improve air circulation.

Red Oak

Planting

Red Oaks thrive in well-drained, loamy soil and full sun. They prefer slightly acidic to neutral soil.

Care

Water young trees regularly. Once established, they are relatively drought-tolerant. Fertilize in early spring and prune to shape as needed.
